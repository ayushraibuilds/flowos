This isn't a todo app. It's a personal operating system for your time. The core insight: productivity tools fail because they're about managing tasks, not managing energy and attention. This app manages all three — tasks, energy, and attention — and rewards you when they align. Flutter (you already know it) means one codebase: iOS personal use today, Android Play Store tomorrow.

The name: FlowOS
What makes this different from every other productivity app
Every todo app makes you feel guilty when you don't finish. FlowOS is designed around flow states — the psychological condition where you're fully absorbed, deeply focused, and the most productive version of yourself. Tasks are assigned "energy levels" (not just priority). The app matches high-energy tasks to your peak hours (tracked over time). It rewards effort, not just completion. A 25-minute deep focus session on a hard problem earns more XP than checking off 10 easy tasks. The AI daily report doesn't judge — it finds patterns and shows you when, where, and how you do your best work.
Core philosophy — 4 pillars
🎯 Intentional Starts
Every day begins with a 2-minute "Daily Intention" — you pick 3 Most Important Tasks (MITs) and set an energy budget. This 2-minute ritual is scientifically proven to increase task completion by 40%.
⚡ Energy-Aware Scheduling
Tasks have energy costs (Deep Work / Light Work / Admin). The app learns your energy patterns over 2 weeks and suggests high-energy tasks during your peak hours. Never schedule a hard decision at 3 PM again.
🏆 Effort-Based Rewards
XP earned by actual time in focus — not by ticking boxes. 25 minutes of unbroken deep work = 50 XP. 10 social media check-ins = -10 XP. The scorecard reflects reality, not self-deception.
🤖 AI Reflection, Not Judgement
Daily Claude report says: "You do your deepest work between 9–11 AM. Your Tuesday afternoons are consistently your weakest. You've completed 89% of tasks you set before 8 AM." Patterns, not lectures.

Sprint 1

📊 AI Daily Productivity Report
Sprint 2
Generated every evening at 9 PM by Claude. Inputs: completed tasks, focus session durations, XP earned/lost, scroll time logged, break behavior, time of day for each activity. 🧠 Break Content Engine
Sprint 2
When a focus session ends → random break content from Claude: (a) A fascinating fact from the field you're working in, (b) A lateral thinking riddle (answer revealed after 60 seconds), (c) A book recommendation with a 3-sentence hook, (d) A 4-7-8 breathing exercise prompt, (e) A micro-creative challenge ("Describe your current task as a movie title"). These make breaks feel earned and mentally refreshing.
📅 Weekly Review
Sprint 3
Every Sunday 8 PM: 5-minute guided review. Claude analyses 7 days of data → generates insights: peak productivity day, average scroll time vs focus time, task completion rate by energy level. You answer 3 reflection questions (pre-set by Claude based on your week). Next week's top habit recommendation generated. Beautiful infographic of the week's data.
⚡ Energy Level Tracker
Sprint 2
Quick check-in widget: "How's your energy?" → 5-point scale, 3 taps. Logged 3× per day (morning, afternoon, evening). After 2 weeks, app shows your energy pattern: "You're typically at 8/10 from 9–11 AM and 4/10 from 2–4 PM." Tasks auto-sorted to your peak hours. This feature alone makes FlowOS more valuable than any other todo app.

The XP system is designed so honest, hard work always beats gaming it. You can't earn XP by checking off fake tasks — only by actual time logged in focus sessions and verified completions. The loss mechanics (negative XP for scrolling) are visible but not punitive — they're honest accounting.

Backend — minimal, what you know
Supabase
Auth (Apple Sign In + email). Cloud sync for tasks and sessions. Realtime subscriptions for cross-device sync. Storage for report images. You know this already.
FastAPI microservice (Railway)
Single endpoint: POST /report/generate. Receives day's data from Flutter → calls Claude API → returns structured report JSON. Also: POST /break/suggest → returns break content. Claude Haiku — cheap, fast.
Local-first architecture
Why Isar (local DB) is the core
All tasks, sessions, XP, and daily logs are stored locally in Isar first. The app works 100% offline. Supabase sync happens in the background. This means: no loading spinners, no "you're offline" errors, instant responses. For a personal productivity app this is essential — you don't want network latency interrupting your focus state.

Three ways to get Flutter apps on your own iPhone without the App Store. Ranked by effort vs stability. For a personal daily-use app: Option 2 (Developer account + direct install) is the right choice.

Option 1 — Free: Xcode direct install (no paid account)
1
Connect iPhone to Mac via USB
Open Xcode → Devices and Simulators → trust your device. Flutter project will target your device.
2
Sign with free Apple ID in Xcode
Xcode → Signing & Capabilities → use your Apple ID as team. No payment needed.
3
flutter run → installs on your device
App installs and runs. Works great. But: expires in 7 days. You must reinstall weekly. Fine for testing. Annoying for daily use.
Option 2 — ₹7,500/yr: Apple Developer Account (recommended)
1
Join Apple Developer Program ($99/yr = ~₹7,500)
developer.apple.com/programs/enroll — 24-48 hours to activate. Required for stable personal use + future App Store.
2
Register your device UDID
Xcode → Devices → add your iPhone UDID to provisioning profile. One-time setup per device.
3
Deploy via Xcode or TestFlight personal
Xcode direct: flutter build ios → open .xcworkspace → Product → Archive → distribute to device. Or TestFlight internal testing: upload once, install anytime, auto-updates. TestFlight is the best option for personal daily use.
4
App never expires, auto-updates via TestFlight
When you update the Flutter code: flutter build ipa → upload to TestFlight → your iPhone auto-prompts to update. This is the ideal personal development workflow.
Android (when you're ready for Play Store)
1
Zero extra code needed (Flutter advantage)
Same Flutter codebase. Run: flutter build apk → install directly on Android phone for testing.
2
Play Store: $25 one-time Google Play Console fee
flutter build appbundle → upload to Play Console → internal testing track first → open testing → production. Much simpler than Apple's process.
Mac requirement
⚠️ You need a Mac to build for iOS
iOS builds require Xcode, which only runs on macOS. If you don't have a Mac: (a) Use a Mac cloud service like MacStadium or Github Codespaces (Mac) for the build step, (b) develop on any machine, build on cloud Mac, (c) or get a MacBook — you'll need one eventually for iOS development anyway. For Android development only: any OS works fine.
